Discipline 1: Priorities - The Problem

What Goes Wrong

In a busy design and build firm, it's easy to:

  • Get bogged down in too many tasks
  • Suffer from scope creep
  • Have teams pulling in different directions
  • Lose focus on critical projects

Result: Energy is diffused, critical projects stall, budgets are exceeded.

Analogy: Imagine designing a stunning restaurant interior. If the design team prioritizes aesthetics over functionality, or the build team deviates on material specifications, the client's core vision is lost. Clear priorities keep everyone aligned to that original vision.

Discipline 1: Priorities - The Solution

Crystal Clear Focus

Define a handful of critical strategic goals for your company:

  • "Expanding into new market sectors"
  • "Improving sustainability practices by X%"
  • "Increasing project margins by Y%"

Crucially: Everyone must know the absolute top 1-3 priorities for the quarter or their specific project.

Discipline 2: Data - The Problem

Decision-Making in the Dark

Many small businesses make decisions based on:

  • Gut feelings
  • Outdated spreadsheets
  • Incomplete information
  • Assumptions rather than facts

In design and build, this leads to:

  • Budget overruns
  • Missed deadlines
  • Client dissatisfaction
  • Unaddressed issues that compound

Discipline 2: Data - The Solution

Disciplined Data Approach

Collect, analyze, and act on key data:

Quantitative Data:

  • Project costs and timelines
  • Resource utilization
  • Quality metrics

Qualitative Feedback:

  • Client satisfaction
  • Sub-contractor performance
  • Site issues and resolution

Discipline 3: Rhythm - The Problem

Chaos Without Cadence

Without consistent meeting and communication rhythm:

  • Priorities get forgotten
  • Data is ignored
  • Accountability evaporates
  • Meetings become unproductive
  • Crucial decisions are delayed
Analogy: Think of a highly efficient construction site. There's a daily morning briefing, weekly progress meetings, and clear communication channels. This rhythm keeps the entire operation flowing smoothly and ensures everyone knows their role and the overall schedule.

Discipline 3: Rhythm - The Solution

Disciplined Cadence

Establish consistent meeting and communication habits:

  • Regular progress reviews
  • Data analysis sessions
  • Informed decision-making
  • Clear accountability at every level

Goal: Create a "beat" that keeps everyone in sync.
